How to Measure Greatness

We rate the greatest Champions League player with a ranking-based UEFA GOAT Score. In every metric, rank #1 gets 100 points, rank #2 gets 95, and every next place loses five points before the weighted total is added.

UEFA GOAT Score

S = max(105 - 5r, 0)

U = 0.25G + 0.15A + 0.20K + 0.15T + 0.10P + 0.10B + 0.05H

G = Goals, A = Assists, K = Knockout G+A, T = Titles, P = Appearances, B = Best XI, H = Peak. Here r is the metric rank, so each lower place loses 5 points.

Goals · 25%

Total Champions League scoring output carries the heaviest weight in the model.

Assists · 15%

Playmaking and direct creation matter because great attackers also raise teammates.

Knockout G+A · 20%

Goals plus assists in the knockout rounds capture performance when pressure is highest.

Titles · 15%

Winning the competition matters, especially when a player turns elite seasons into trophies.

Appearances · 10%

Long runs in Europe reward durability, consistency, and relevance across many seasons.

UEFA Best XI · 10%

Selection counts add a recognition signal for how often a player ranked among Europe’s best.

Peak · 5%

Peak reflects the highest single-season level a player reached in the Champions League.
